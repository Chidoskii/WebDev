History of Sports

     The first accounts of sporting events ever taking place goes back about 3,000 years. The Olympic Games first took place in 776 BC and hosted many events such as wrestling, jumping, discus and javelin throwing, and chariot races. The ancient Greeks are said to have introduced formal sports to the world. The ancient Games took place from 776 BC to 393 AD. In 1894, a Frenchman named Baron Pierre de Coubertin proposed the idea of reinstating the Olympic Games. Two years later, the first modern Olympics were held in Athens, Greece.

History of Football

     Modern football originated in Britain during the 1800's. Folk style football had been played since Midieval times. Each town or village would play their style of football with their own set of rules. In 1863, rules of football were published which prohibited the the use of handling the ball. These rules were created by the Football Association, which is the ruling body for English football.

History of Basketball

     James Naismith is the father of basketball. Naismith was a gym teacher in Springfield, Massachusetts. One day, it was raining and Naismith wanted to do an indoor activity that will keep his students active. He came up with a game where players would pass a ball amongst teammates to try and score points by placing the ball into a peach basket mounted on a wall at a certain height. Today, basketball is one of the most popular and widely viewed sports.

History of Mixed Martial Arts

     MMA is a full contanct combat sport that incorporates striking, grappling, and ground fighting. Howard Rosenberg is credited as being the first person to use the term 'mixed martial arts'. Interstylistic contests in Japan as well as Vale Tudo in Brazil were the main inspiration for early mixed martial arts. It is said that an exhibition bout between boxer Muhammad Ali and wrestler Antonio Inoki inspired the foundation of Shooto, Pancrase, and Pride Fighting Championship. Today, the leader in comnbat sports is the Ultimate Fighting Championship.
